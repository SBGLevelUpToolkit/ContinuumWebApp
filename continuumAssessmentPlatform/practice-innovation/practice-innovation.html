Traveller

Work distribution relies heavily on formal documentation.

Transparency is inexistent and team members do what they are told to without any knowledge for the bigger picture.

Role of QA is solely to perform testing to find defects and bugs in the product.

There are no records of any testing done.

Handover is informal and not documented.

Once-off informal in-house training conducted.

Customers are not in the loop when it comes to product development and progress.

Kanban - not release bounded or Scrum - not time boxed properly due to lack of proper planning.

No set structure or guidelines for User stories.

Code packages are not shippable at the end of releases.

Written tests are not programming adaptive (i.e. cannot be coded/automated).

Artisan

Work distribution is done based on strategic planning and regular sync-up takes place to provide updates on WIP.

QA's input is valued in the design phase of the product.

Handover is documented and available but not maintained.

Training requirement is identified regularly.

Consistent training process for new team members is in place.

Expert

Teams adhere to the agile principles and values.

Formal ceremonies in place to promote communication amongst the teams and through all levels of the enterprise but not done regularly.

Pair Programming and Test First Approach is favoured and more focus is put on defect prevention.

All QA team members have basic knowledge of performance, security and other NFT and applies their knowledge when promoting quality throughout the process.

Feature teams are able to provide coverage reports and record of all testing and QA activities done upon request.

Formal handover documentation continuously reviewed and updated.

Updating the formal documentation is part of Definition of Done for continuity purposes.

Formal training is arranged and continuously revised and adhered to market demand.

Use of Personas based on real customer profiles.

Kanban - release bounded or scrum - time boxed properly to align with company's vision and product releases (i.e. teams apply Cadence and Synchronise with other feature teams).

All code packages are shippable at the end of a release.

Professional

Daily stand-ups (concise and time framed) takes place where team members gives quick feedback and their goals for the day.

Scrum of scrum meetings takes place, where all important information are filtered down to each feature team (transparency and knowledge of the big picture).

QA is fully cross-skilled with knowledge of business, development, functional and non functional testing.

All activities should adhere to a set list of Definition of Readiness and Definition of Done.

Regular occurrence of Showcase involving Product owners and other feature teams.

Sprint Demo, Product Increment Demo are used to showcase successful delivery of the User Story/Function point to Business before releases/implementations.

Knowledge sharing sessions are happening proactively within team members.

Team members are given the opportunity to go to workshops/training to upskill themselves.

Test First approach is adopted and all written test are in a programming adaptive manner.

Proper structure and guidelines set for User Stories based on criteria such as Description, functionality and time estimation (estimation is optional but good to have).

Master

Feature Teams adopts methods (like the happiness metrics and team health checks) to promote individual and team productivity.
