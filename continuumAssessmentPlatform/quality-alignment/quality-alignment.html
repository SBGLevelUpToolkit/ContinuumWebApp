Traveller

There are no common practices nor rules in place for the team.

Testing is a phase after development.

There is no plan on how to test increment.

Testing (Functional and Non-Functional) is not involved in PI and/or Release Planning.

There is no governance structure whatsoever.

Agenda not clearly defined nor understood by individuals throughout the different levels of SAFE.

Feature teams do not have a clear understanding of the roles of the people they deal with.

Customer feedback do not reach the team levels.

Testing team defines all the work to be done along with Dev and business without involving Specialist team.

Artisan

All written scripts are reviewed by whole team and follows standards that is agreed by the team.

Each QE/Test Analyst plans his/her own individual approach to testing their work increment.

No formal Test strategy is in place, and any alignment to any such strategy is purely coincidental.

Alignment between different feature teams and product road map is inexistent.

External team responsible for governance.

Top-down Information is sifted and communicated on a need to know basis to feature team levels.

Expert

Quality standards documented and in line with Quality Policy of the organization.

Testing activities are discussed and defined with estimation/sizing of user stories.

Team is aligned on testing effort required per user story and of the plan on how to test.

Testing and QA is shifted-left by making use of Test First Approach to build quality in.

Test strategy is defined upfront with all levels of testing being considered.

Specialist teams are involved upfront.

Fit for purpose sign-offs (Go Ahead) for all product quality enhancement proposals.

Team tries to build in customer feedback loop but is not always actioned or incorporated in planning.

Proper chain of communication established to pass down information from portfolio level to all levels but still not a 2 way communication.

Specialist team involved from system architecture planning (including NFT Teams).

Professional

Standards match and align to the Bank's Standards for Quality.

Quality Assurance follows a high level standard to ensure quality increment delivery at the end of each sprint.

Test plan and strategy for all partner feature teams are in line with Product Roadmap.

Defects, Incidents and risk management and mitigation plans/process is in place and understood by all.

Fit for purpose governance makes it an enabler rather than a hindrance.

Full transparency - All decisions and strategy discussion is understood by all individuals at all levels in the Scaled Agile framework. (Portfolio, program and team levels).

All feature teams working in parallel have a clear view and understanding of what each team does and have their goals aligned based on release and product deadlines. (vision is aligned with iterations and releases.).

Scrum of scrum takes place to provide feedback and as a communication channel to ensure goals alignment and information delivered across all levels.

Testers are fully involved with Developers as part of the Test First Approach to help build the product better.

With T-shaped skills Testers can play an advisory role from both a business and testing perspective.

Testers are the intermediary to help communication between DEV and Business where necessary.

Master

Test Plan, Strategy and Process is adaptive and easily responsive to change.

Team is able to evolve testing as the product and team dynamics matures.

QA leads are fully engaged with business and Development leads in making product related decisions.

Governance values team throughput and value add.

Customer feedback is acknowledged, given priority and taken into consideration when planning releases and iterations.

The feature teams consists of Operational team members and the whole team is responsible for the product quality, from the inception to maintenance in production "You build it, you run it".
